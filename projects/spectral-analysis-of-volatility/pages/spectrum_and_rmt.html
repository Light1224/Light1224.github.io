Spectrum and RMT

This post focuses on separating structural eigenmodes from random-matrix bulk behavior.

A scree curve alone can look persuasive in almost any high-dimensional dataset. The key step is to place MP bounds and histogram overlays next to that curve and test whether observed outliers are truly beyond finite-sample noise expectations.

Across baseline runs, the same pattern appears: one dominant mode, one meaningful secondary mode, and a long tail that mostly sits inside bulk-like range. Report metrics mirror this pattern through small signal-mode counts and concentrated signal mass near the top of the spectrum.

The first two figures are compact legacy references; the next two are baseline phase-12 exports from the artifact pipeline.

Important caveat

Pairwise-overlap estimation can produce indefinite sample matrices, so strict Wishart assumptions are not perfectly satisfied in every window. MP is therefore used as a robust filtering heuristic with diagnostics, not as a strict theorem claim in this empirical context.

How this changed the workflow

After this stage, the workflow treats retained signal modes and bulk modes differently in every later step: interpretation, reconstruction, regime modeling, and predictive diagnostics.
