Spectrum and RMT

Entry: this is the part where I separate structure from random-matrix bulk.

I like this phase because it forces me to stop hand-waving. A scree plot by itself can always look meaningful. But once I put MP bounds and histogram overlays beside it, I can ask a harder question: what would this spectrum look like if it were mostly random finite-sample noise?

In my baseline setup I repeatedly got the same shape: one massive mode, one secondary mode, and a long tail that sits mostly in bulk-like territory. In the findings and report metrics this is reflected as small signal mode count with meaningful signal mass concentration in the top part of the spectrum.

Caveat I do not ignore

Pairwise-overlap estimation can produce indefinite sample matrices, so strict Wishart assumptions are never perfectly satisfied. I treat MP as a robust heuristic filter with diagnostics, not as a courtroom-proof theorem in this empirical setup.

How this changed my workflow

After this phase I stopped discussing “all factors” as if they were equally meaningful. I explicitly treat the retained signal modes and the bulk modes differently in interpretation, reconstruction, and predictive tests.
